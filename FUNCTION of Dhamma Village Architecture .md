##  **FUNCTION AND ROLE OF THE DHAMMA HALL**

### **(Central Facility of the Dhamma Village)** {#central-facility-of-the-dhamma-village .unnumbered}

##  {#section .unnumbered}

## **1. POSITIONING: WHY THE DHAMMA HALL IS AT THE CENTER** {#positioning-why-the-dhamma-hall-is-at-the-center .unnumbered}

The Dhamma Hall is located at the **physical center** of the Dhamma
Village to serve as the **functional and psychological anchor** of the
entire settlement.

Its central position ensures:

-   Equal accessibility from all residential and functional zones

-   No association with authority, ownership, or hierarchy

-   A shared reference point for daily routine and discipline

**Important clarification:\
** The central location does **not** represent power or control.\
It represents **shared discipline and collective orientation**.

## **2. PRIMARY FUNCTION OF THE DHAMMA HALL** {#primary-function-of-the-dhamma-hall .unnumbered}

### **A. DAILY STRUCTURED PRACTICE SPACE** {#a.-daily-structured-practice-space .unnumbered}

The Dhamma Hall provides a dedicated, neutral environment for **daily
group meditation sessions**, conducted at fixed times.

Role:

-   Supports regularity and continuity of practice

-   Creates a calm, distraction-free environment

-   Reinforces discipline through routine rather than instruction

This function is **preventive**, not therapeutic.

### **B. COMMUNITY DISCIPLINE ANCHOR** {#b.-community-discipline-anchor .unnumbered}

The hall functions as the **time-keeper of village life**.

-   Daily activities across the village are structured around hall
    timings

-   Work, meals, rest, and learning align with a common rhythm

-   The hall indirectly organizes village life without issuing commands

This prevents chaos and fragmentation common in collective living
systems.

## **3. SOCIAL FUNCTION** {#social-function .unnumbered}

### **A. COMMON, NON-OWNED SPACE** {#a.-common-non-owned-space .unnumbered}

The Dhamma Hall belongs to:

-   No individual

-   No group

-   No authority

It is the **only space equally shared by all residents**, regardless of
role or background.

This:

-   Reduces social hierarchy

-   Encourages equality and inclusion

-   Prevents dominance by any subgroup

### **B. SPACE FOR COLLECTIVE SILENCE AND COHESION** {#b.-space-for-collective-silence-and-cohesion .unnumbered}

Unlike meeting halls that promote discussion, the Dhamma Hall:

-   Promotes shared silence

-   Reduces verbal conflict

-   Builds non-verbal social cohesion

This has a stabilizing effect on interpersonal relationships across the
village.

## **4. OPERATIONAL ROLE IN THE VILLAGE SYSTEM** {#operational-role-in-the-village-system .unnumbered}

### **A. BEHAVIORAL REGULATION (INDIRECT)** {#a.-behavioral-regulation-indirect .unnumbered}

The hall does **not** enforce rules.\
Instead, it:

-   Creates an environment where calm behavior becomes normal

-   Reduces impulsivity and emotional reactivity

-   Improves self-regulation among residents

This lowers the need for external supervision.

### **B. CONFLICT PREVENTION** {#b.-conflict-prevention .unnumbered}

Regular use of the hall:

-   Reduces accumulated stress

-   Improves emotional awareness

-   Lowers frequency and intensity of conflicts

The hall therefore functions as a **preventive social infrastructure**,
not just a building.

## **5. ROLE IN GOVERNANCE & DECISION-MAKING** {#role-in-governance-decision-making .unnumbered}

The Dhamma Hall is **not used for administration or authority**.

Rules:

-   No political discussions

-   No decision-making meetings

-   No announcements or directives

Administrative activities are deliberately kept **outside** the hall.

This separation ensures:

-   Psychological safety

-   Neutrality

-   Non-coercive participation

## **6. ROLE FOR DIFFERENT USER GROUPS** {#role-for-different-user-groups .unnumbered}

### **A. Service Residents** {#a.-service-residents .unnumbered}

-   Anchor for daily discipline

-   Reinforces responsibility and consistency

### **B. Householders & Short-Term Participants** {#b.-householders-short-term-participants .unnumbered}

-   Helps maintain focus while continuing daily work

-   Acts as a stabilizing "second home" reference

### **C. Children & Youth** {#c.-children-youth .unnumbered}

-   Indirect exposure to calm routines

-   Learning by observation, not instruction

### **D. Visiting Practitioners** {#d.-visiting-practitioners .unnumbered}

-   Deepens practice without isolation from community life

## **7. ARCHITECTURAL IMPLICATIONS (FUNCTION-DRIVEN)** {#architectural-implications-function-driven .unnumbered}

Because of its role, the Dhamma Hall must be:

-   Simple in form

-   Free of ornamentation

-   Naturally ventilated

-   Acoustically calm

-   Large enough for collective sitting

-   Visually central but not monumental

**Explicitly avoided:**

-   Religious symbols

-   Decorative dominance

-   Elevated platforms that imply authority

## **8. WHY THE DHAMMA HALL IS CRITICAL TO THE MODEL** {#why-the-dhamma-hall-is-critical-to-the-model .unnumbered}

Without the Dhamma Hall:

-   The village becomes only a residential campus

-   Discipline becomes optional and fragmented

-   Social cohesion weakens

-   Preventive mental well-being collapses

With the Dhamma Hall:

-   Discipline becomes habitual

-   Community rhythm stabilizes

-   Individual behavior improves organically

-   The village functions as a **living system**, not a housing project

## **9. SUMMARY (FOR DPR / CSR USE)** {#summary-for-dpr-csr-use .unnumbered}

The Dhamma Hall is the **central functional infrastructure** of the
Dhamma Village.\
It provides a shared space for daily mental discipline, anchors the
rhythm of village life, supports emotional regulation, and strengthens
social cohesion. By operating without authority, symbolism, or
ownership, it enables preventive mental well-being through environment
and routine rather than instruction or enforcement.

**THE DOME (DHAMMA HALL) IS SURROUNDED BY MONK RESIDENCES**

## **1. FUNCTIONAL PROXIMITY, NOT AUTHORITY** {#functional-proximity-not-authority .unnumbered}

Monk residences are located around the Dhamma Hall **not to signify
power or hierarchy**, but to ensure **functional proximity** to the core
practice space.

-   Monks use the hall most frequently for guidance, meditation, and
    reflection

-   Immediate access reduces movement, noise, and disruption

-   The hall remains active and supervised without formal control

This is **logistical efficiency**, not symbolic dominance.

## **2. CONTINUITY OF DISCIPLINE AROUND THE CORE** {#continuity-of-discipline-around-the-core .unnumbered}

The presence of monk residences around the Dhamma Hall creates a
**continuous zone of calm and discipline**.

-   Surrounding residences act as a buffer against noise from outer
    village areas

-   The central zone remains consistently quiet throughout the day

-   The hall is protected from becoming a transit or shortcut space

This spatial arrangement preserves the **psychological sanctity** of the
hall without enforcement.

## **3. PASSIVE STEWARDSHIP, NOT GOVERNANCE** {#passive-stewardship-not-governance .unnumbered}

Monks are **not administrators** of the village.\
Their proximity to the Dhamma Hall enables **passive stewardship**, not
control.

-   They do not issue instructions or manage operations

-   Their presence naturally encourages respectful use of the space

-   The hall remains orderly without rules being spoken or enforced

This reduces the need for guards, signage, or supervision.

## **4. CLEAR SPATIAL HIERARCHY OF PRACTICE VS DAILY LIFE** {#clear-spatial-hierarchy-of-practice-vs-daily-life .unnumbered}

By placing monk residences closest to the Dhamma Hall:

-   The **innermost ring** is reserved for deep practice and quiet
    living

-   Service residents and householders are placed in outer rings

-   Work, utilities, and social activity are pushed further outward

This creates a **natural gradient from silence to activity**, which is
essential in a disciplined community.

## **5. PROTECTION OF THE DOME FROM SOCIAL PRESSURE** {#protection-of-the-dome-from-social-pressure .unnumbered}

If the Dhamma Hall were surrounded by:

-   Householders, or

-   Guest accommodation, or

-   Activity buildings

it would quickly become:

-   Socialized

-   Politicized

-   Distracted

Monk residences act as a **protective ring**, ensuring the hall remains:

-   Neutral

-   Non-social

-   Non-administrative

## **6. ROLE MODELLING THROUGH LIVING, NOT TEACHING** {#role-modelling-through-living-not-teaching .unnumbered}

Monks surrounding the Dhamma Hall provide **silent role modelling**.

-   Discipline is demonstrated through daily routine

-   No instruction or preaching is required

-   Residents observe consistency, simplicity, and restraint

This supports behavior change through **environmental influence**, not
authority.

## **7. TEMPORARY PRESENCE, NOT PERMANENT CONTROL** {#temporary-presence-not-permanent-control .unnumbered}

Important clarification for officials:

-   Monks do **not** permanently occupy a single village

-   Residences allow **rotational or temporary stays\
    **

-   No monk owns or controls the hall or surrounding space

This prevents institutionalization and concentration of influence.

## **8. ARCHITECTURAL & OPERATIONAL BENEFITS** {#architectural-operational-benefits .unnumbered}

Surrounding the dome with monk residences:

-   Reduces foot traffic across the hall

-   Maintains consistent silence zones

-   Simplifies access control

-   Improves safety and order without signage or enforcement

It is a **low-cost, low-conflict spatial solution**.

## **9. SUMMARY (ONE-PARAGRAPH VERSION)** {#summary-one-paragraph-version .unnumbered}

The Dhamma Hall is surrounded by monk residences to maintain functional
proximity, continuity of discipline, and environmental calm around the
village's central practice space. This arrangement provides passive
stewardship, protects the hall from social and operational pressures,
and creates a clear spatial gradient from silence to activity. The
design supports preventive mental well-being through structure and
routine rather than authority or enforcement, while ensuring monks
remain guides rather than administrators.

**MONK RESIDENCES ARE SURROUNDED BY DHAMMASEVAK RESIDENCES**

## **1. BUFFER, NOT BARRIER** {#buffer-not-barrier .unnumbered}

**Dhamma-sevak residences form a functional buffer zone between monk
residences and the rest of the village.**

-   **Monks require a consistently quiet, disciplined living
    environment\
    **

-   **The broader village includes work, movement, and daily activity\
    **

-   **Dhamma-sevak residences absorb this transition\
    **

**This prevents sudden exposure to noise, visitors, or operational
activity near monk residences.**

-   **Create social pressure\
    **

    **

-   **Blur boundaries of practice\
    **

-   **They understand discipline and routines\
    **

-   **They respect silence and timing\
    **

-   **They act as a living interface, not a wall\
    **

**This keeps monks accessible but not disturbed.**

**Dhamma-sevaks are responsible for:**

-   **Daily operational support\
    **

-   **Coordination of schedules\
    **

-   **Maintaining discipline and order\
    **

-   **Handling practical needs\
    **

**Placing their residences around monk housing ensures:**

-   **Quick response without formal authority\
    **

-   **Minimal movement across the village\
    **

-   **Reduced need for external staff or supervision\
    **

**This is efficiency by design, not hierarchy.**

## **4. ROLE CLARITY AND BOUNDARY MAINTENANCE** {#role-clarity-and-boundary-maintenance .unnumbered}

**The spatial order establishes clear role separation:**

-   **Monks: guidance, practice, reflection (no administration)\
    **

-   **Dhamma-sevaks: facilitation, service, operational continuity\
    **

-   **Householders/guests: participation without responsibility for core

**By physically surrounding monk residences with dhamma-sevaks:**

    **

**Dhamma-sevaks live closest to monks because they:**

-   **Have already accepted disciplined living\

-   **Inner core: monks\

-   **Outer ring: general residents\

-   **Monks are shielded from unregulated access\
    **

-   **Visitors do not directly approach monk residences\

-   **Dhamma-sevaks ensure accountability without policing\

**This arrangement reduces:**

-   **Social misunderstandings\
    **

    **

-   **Need for formal enforcement mechanisms\
    **

## **7. TEMPORARY MONASTIC PRESENCE, STABLE SUPPORT SYSTEM** {#temporary-monastic-presence-stable-support-system .unnumbered}

**Monks do not permanently reside in one village.\
Dhamma-sevaks provide continuity.**

-   **Monks rotate or stay temporarily\
    **

-   **Dhamma-sevaks maintain long-term stability\
    **

-   **The system does not collapse when monks change\

**Surrounding monk residences with dhamma-sevaks ensures institutional
